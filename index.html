FAQ

What is Save the Children?
Save the Children is an international non-profit founded by Eglantyne Jebb, over 100 years ago. We are active in over 100 countries and were the world’s first international charity for children. We give children in the U.S. and around the world a healthy start in life, the opportunity to learn and protection from harm. When a crisis occurs, we are among the first to respond to an emergency and the last to leave.Because of people like you, we’re able to make a difference in children’s lives all around the world.
How do I apply for a job at Save the Children?
Save the Children is India’s leading independent child rights NGO. It works in 19 Indian states as of December 2018 and has changed the lives of 14 million Indian children since 2008. Our programmes are implemented in the remotest corners of India and urban areas to provide quality education and healthcare, protection from harm and abuse and life-saving aid during emergencies to children. A major aspect of Save the Children’s work is to engage with and empower the most marginalised communities of India. We take children’s voice to the platforms where it matters the most. We engage with the Government to drive policy change in the best interest of children. Globally, Save the Children is present in more than 80 countries and works to improve the situation of children living there. We're poised to reach 4 million of the most deprived children of India in the perio 2019-2021. Visit www.savethechildren.in to know more.
How do I volunteer for a specific emergency?
When accidents and disasters occur, the first persons who normally appear at the scene are citizens. Volunteer citizens with the skills and local knowledge can play important roles when accidents and disasters occur and can provide valuable assistance to the relief forces.
